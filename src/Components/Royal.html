Standing along the banks of the Mekong River in the heart of Phnom Penh, the Royal Palace is a magnificent symbol of Cambodia’s monarchy, culture, and architectural heritage. Built in the 19th century, this elegant complex serves as the official residence of the Cambodian King and showcases a blend of traditional Khmer design with influences from classical architecture. With its golden spires, grand throne hall, and the nearby Silver Pagoda housing precious religious treasures, the Royal Palace offers visitors a glimpse into Cambodia’s royal traditions and spiritual identity.

Food and Culture

Food

Phnom Penh Chicken Rice

A beloved local dish in Cambodia’s capital, Phnom Penh chicken rice features tender, flavorful chicken served with fragrant steamed rice cooked with aromatic spices and chicken broth. Often accompanied by fresh cucumber, herbs, pickled vegetables, and a savory dipping sauce, the dish offers a comforting balance of rich, savory, and refreshing flavors. Popular among locals and visitors alike, it represents the simple yet delicious street food culture found throughout Phnom Penh.

Kuy Teav

A traditional Cambodian noodle soup and one of the most popular breakfast dishes in Phnom Penh, Kuy Teav is made with smooth rice noodles served in a rich, aromatic broth. The soup is commonly topped with sliced pork, beef, seafood, or meatballs, along with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime, and chili for added flavor. Known for its comforting taste and delicate balance of savory and fresh ingredients, Kuy Teav reflects Cambodia’s everyday food culture and is a must-try dish for visitors exploring the capital.

Culture

Khmer Art and Craftsmanship

The palace displays Cambodia’s rich artistic heritage through detailed murals, sculptures, carvings, and traditional decorations. The craftsmanship reflects the skills of Khmer artists and the country’s long history of architectural excellence.

Angkor Wat — General Day Flow

Time of Day What to Do
Early Morning Arrive at the Royal Palace early to enjoy the peaceful atmosphere before larger crowds arrive. The morning light is also ideal for viewing the palace’s golden roofs and detailed architecture.
Morning Begin exploring the Royal Palace complex, including the Throne Hall and royal buildings. Learn about Cambodia’s monarchy, royal traditions, and the important role the palace plays in national identity.
Late-Morning Visit the Silver Pagoda, one of Cambodia’s most important Buddhist sites. Admire its sacred Buddha statues, valuable treasures, and beautiful artwork that reflect the country’s spiritual heritage.
Midday Take a break at a nearby café or restaurant and experience Phnom Penh’s local food culture. Try traditional dishes such as Kuy Teav, Phnom Penh chicken rice, or other Khmer specialties.
Early Afternoon Continue exploring the surrounding area, including nearby riverside streets and cultural landmarks. Enjoy views along the Mekong River and observe the daily life of the capital city.
Mid-Afternoon Visit local markets or souvenir shops to discover Cambodian handicrafts, traditional products, and gifts. This is a good opportunity to experience the creativity and craftsmanship of local communities.
Late Afternoon Return near the palace area to enjoy the changing atmosphere as the city becomes more active. The riverside area is a relaxing place to take photos and appreciate Phnom Penh’s urban landscape.
Evening End the day with dinner at a local restaurant or along the riverside. Enjoy Cambodian cuisine while reflecting on the history, architecture, and cultural experiences from the palace visit.
