Destinations

Cambodia's most famous sites span ancient temple cities, sacred mountains, a riverside capital, and tropical islands. Each destination below offers a distinct reason to visit, from world heritage architecture to quiet beaches.

Siemreap Province

Angkor Wat

Built in the early 12th century under King Suryavarman II, Angkor Wat is the largest religious monument in the world and the spiritual centerpiece of the wider Angkor Archaeological Park. Visitors can climb to the upper terrace, walk the long causeway at sunrise, and study the kilometer of carved bas-reliefs depicting Hindu mythology and historical battles.

Most travelers pair Angkor Wat with nearby Angkor Thom and Ta Prohm as part of a multi-day temple pass.

Kampong Spue Province

Oudong Mountain

Oudong served as the royal capital of Cambodia for over two hundred years before Phnom Penh. Today the hilltop is a pilgrimage site dotted with stupas, including one believed to contain a relic of the Buddha. The climb rewards visitors with panoramic views over the surrounding rice plains.

The site is quieter than Angkor, making it a good half-day trip for travelers who want a more contemplative temple visit.

Phnom Penh City

Royal Palace

Cambodia's capital sits at the confluence of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers. The Royal Palace, Silver Pagoda, and National Museum sit close to the riverside promenade, while the Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum offers an essential, sobering look at the country's recent history. Markets like Central Market and Russian Market are well suited to an afternoon of wandering.

Sihanoukville Province

Koh Rong

A short ferry ride from Sihanoukville, Koh Rong is Cambodia's best-known island getaway. Long stretches of white sand line the western coast, while smaller coves further north offer quieter, less developed beaches. Some bays are known for bioluminescent plankton that glows in the water after sunset.
