Travel Tips

Getting around Cambodia is easier than most first-time visitors expect. Here's what to know about transportation, budgeting, and timing your trip before you land.

✈️

Getting There

Most international travelers arrive through Phnom Penh International Airport or Siem Reap Angkor International Airport. E-visas can be arranged online before arrival, or a visa on arrival is available at both airports for most nationalities.

🛺

Getting Around

Tuk-tuks and Grab (the regional ride-hailing app) cover short city trips. For longer distances between provinces, buses and private van transfers are affordable and widely available, while domestic flights connect Phnom Penh and Siem Reap in under an hour.

💵

Money & Costs

The US Dollar is used alongside the Cambodian Riel, and most prices in tourist areas are quoted in USD. ATMs are common in cities, but it's worth carrying small bills for markets, tuk-tuks, and rural areas.

☀️

Best Time to Visit

The dry season from November to April offers the most comfortable weather for temple visits and island trips. The wet season from May to October brings lush green landscapes and fewer crowds, with short afternoon showers.

🙏

Local Etiquette

Modest clothing covering shoulders and knees is expected at temples. Removing shoes before entering homes and pagodas, and greeting with the traditional "sampeah" palms-together gesture, are both appreciated by locals.

🩺

Health & Safety

Bottled or filtered water is recommended over tap water. Standard travel vaccinations and mosquito protection are advised, and travel insurance is strongly recommended for motorbike or island activities.
